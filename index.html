McLaren Engineering — 2025 / 26

mLSD Clutch Pack
Sizing Tool

Developed McLaren Engineering's first internal design capability for clutch-type limited-slip differentials from the ground up — zero prior institutional knowledge existed for this product type. The tool covers the complete design chain: bevel gear geometry, clutch plate sizing, torque bias and torque-to-turn prediction, stack length packaging, and adiabatic thermal analysis for slip events.

A key outcome was an original closed-form TTT derivation that includes a gear-thrust self-actuation correction term not identified in any existing published source — a factor empirically confirmed as significant during open differential testing. The tool was presented to the McLaren Technical Review Board.

McLaren Engineering — 2025

Bevel Gear Backlash
Measurement Fixture

Designed and commissioned a custom high-stiffness test fixture to measure gear mesh backlash within ±0.01mm repeatability. This tool was required to validate bevel gear tooth contact patterns under varying shim conditions, directly supporting the calibration of an active differential prototype.

Project included full SolidWorks assembly design, FEA stiffness verification to ensure fixture deflection did not contaminate dial indicator readings, and coordination with local machine shops for CNC fabrication.

McLaren Engineering — 2025

Advanced Coating &
Substrate DOE Program

Lead Engineer for a multi-phase Design of Experiments (DOE) investigating the friction and wear characteristics of novel non-standard coatings (DLC, PVD, and specialty polymers) for differential thrust washers. This was a critical response to upcoming European environmental legislation requiring the elimination of traditional PTFE/PFAS-based material protocols.

Phase 3 was explicitly designed to answer three engineering hypotheses. Full program ownership included test plan design, procurement coordination across 5+ suppliers, sample serialization, cost matrix development, and test requisition authoring through all phases.

McLaren Engineering — 2025 / 26

9T65 Open Differential
Pursuit — Design & Drawings

Drove iterative CAD and engineering drawing development across an extended OEM pursuit program for a next-generation automatic transmission differential. Managed all model and drawing revisions in SolidWorks and Teamcenter through multiple competitive design cycles — from early-stage REV2B configurations through the final REV3 gear and case design.

Deliverables included gear drawings, assembly model updates, BOM maintenance, a design tolerances reference sheet for manufacturing handoff, and a VCRI document capturing interface and design input requirements. Authored all four surrogate test phase reports and presented formal results to the OEM customer in a structured engineering review.
