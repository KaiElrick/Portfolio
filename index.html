McLaren Engineering — 2025 / 26

mLSD Clutch Pack
Sizing Tool

Developed McLaren Engineering's first internal design capability for clutch-type limited-slip differentials from the ground up — zero prior institutional knowledge existed for this product type. The tool covers the complete design chain: bevel gear geometry, clutch plate sizing, torque bias and torque-to-turn prediction, stack length packaging, and adiabatic thermal analysis for slip events.

A key outcome was an original closed-form TTT derivation that includes a gear-thrust self-actuation correction term not identified in any existing published source — a factor empirically confirmed as significant during open differential testing. The tool was presented to the McLaren Technical Review Board.

McLaren Engineering — 2025 / 26

Universal Bevel Gear
Test Fixture

Designed a modular, multi-program bevel gear test fixture to drastically reduce the cost and lead time of testing differential internals across new OEM programs. The fixture is compatible with three test stand configurations — 2-axis dyno, 3-axis dyno, and static impact stand — eliminating the need for a dedicated fixture per program.

Full design scope included bolt calculations (assembly, cover, dyno interface), FEA validation of major structural components, GD&T, DFM improvements, interchangeable inserts for different gearset geometries, and re-centering within the dyno envelope.

McLaren Engineering — 2025 / 26

4-Phase Differential
Surrogate Test Program

Championed a 4-phase structured DOE score test program for a major transmission pursuit, using a production differential as a surrogate for a higher-torque next-generation design. The program spans 1400–2500 Nm against OEM test specifications and systematically isolates five independent design variables: case surface treatment, cross shaft coating, pinion gear geometry, washer material, and lubrication protocol.

Phase 3 was explicitly designed to answer three engineering hypotheses. Full program ownership included test plan design, procurement coordination across 5+ suppliers, sample serialization, cost matrix development, and test requisition authoring through all phases.

06 — McLaren Engineering

Cross Shaft Coating
Tribology Study

Led post-test analysis and lessons learned documentation for a tribometer DOE conducted at an external university materials lab (ASTM G133, Anton-Paar TRB3). 7 cross shaft variants tested across 4 coating types and 3 suppliers. Developed a weighted multi-metric ranking methodology to synthesize performance across friction coefficient, wear rate, surface hardness, and elastic modulus.

  • DLC drastically outperformed all coatings across every metric
  • Supplier effect was significant and independent of coating type (QPQ-Kang Ni showed 77% smaller wear track vs. Showa)
  • Average kinetic friction coefficient (μk) identified as best single predictor of performance
  • Concluded: simple tribological tests cannot yet reliably predict differential seizure — surrogate testing required

C-Arm Fluoroscopy
Training Simulator

Engineered a cost-effective, 3D-printable miniature replica of a clinical C-arm fluoroscopy device for medical training. Accelerometer data interfaces with imaging software to dynamically adjust a simulated X-ray projection angle as the device is repositioned — teaching clinicians proper technique without using live equipment.

08 — Translational Biophotonics Lab · NSERC USRA

Low-Cost NIR Brain
Oxygenation Spectrometer

Designed and built a prototype near-infrared spectrometer for brain oxygenation monitoring using a novel compressive sensing approach. By replacing a conventional multi-element detector array with a digital micromirror device (DMD) and a single silicon photodiode, the system achieved a 99% reduction in sensor cost versus commercial alternatives.

  • DMD + single photodiode architecture enabling compressive spectral reconstruction
  • Custom PCB designed in Fusion 360 for photodiode-to-Arduino signal conditioning
  • Dual-Arduino system for synchronized DMD pattern generation and signal sampling
  • Funded by NSERC Frugal Biomedical Innovation Research Award (USRA)

09 — Fluidesign Composites · Co-op 2024

Forged Carbon Fiber
Fin & Mold System

Developed a compression-molded "forged" carbon fiber fin to replace the stamped aluminum fin on competitive rowing shells. The process uses shredded manufacturing offcuts as feedstock — eliminating waste while producing a net-shape molded part without pre-preg layup.

  • Designed part geometry and complete 2-piece compression mold in SolidWorks
  • 3D-printed functional prototypes for fit and process validation ahead of tooling
  • Hand laid up and compression molded proof-of-concept parts to verify process
  • Circular manufacturing approach: offcut feedstock reduces material waste
